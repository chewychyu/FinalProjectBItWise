Meditation:



The earlist evidence that our ancestors praticed meditation comes from both Taoist China and Buddhist India, found to be written as early as 400-600BCE, the year where Meiolania aka Vanatu Horned Turtles still exist.

In this article, writer chyu explains her experience in grasping consciousness or mindfulness and how it works to calm one self(self does not exist in buddhism but we can cover ths topic later) and help one to clear their mind if not achieve the amata consciousness, fundamental pure consciousness. Surprisingly enough, this is not about a religion.

Before you skip, meditation is not about relaxing and it is also not about thinking nothing. All you need to do is to be aware that you're thinking. To be aware of surroundings, of your thoughts, of your Dukha(sufferings), of your Sukka(pleasure).

Head stand. Where do I even start:

First off, this pratice named Sirsasana use core, head, neck, and elbows' strength to perform. Personally, I would say the position of head is the most important thing then comes the core. The easiest explanation is beacuse once your neck snap there is no way back. Yes, as scary as it sounds, it is an advanced pratice. You are not recommended to perform this alone without a yogi or yoga instructor with experience to support/aide you.

So, Chyu, you may ask, why did you choose this as the second article to write instead of covering the pratices everyone can follow along. There are two answers, download limit of my Freepik account ran out after downloading different sizes of headstand which gives me with no choices. Second answer is that this is the pratice or pose I enjoy doing the most, apart from the child pose, ofcourse. The moment your legs are up in the air, you will think of nothing but the balance you will have to keep throughout the whole period you are performing this pratice. Minding the thing you are doing right now. I will call it a form of meditation too.
